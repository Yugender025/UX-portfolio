The Problem in my hometown of Hyderabad HYD

On any given day, over 3,300,000 people travel to reach their destinations . The Telangana government has increased the number of buses to over 6000. However, in metropolitan cities, traffic congestion is a major issue, making it difficult to estimate the accurate arrival times of buses at their designated stations. This lack of predictability plays a significant role in everyone's life. Therefore, we have taken these considerations into account to resolve this issue.

However, it is essential for the to have access to the public precise location of buses so that people can plan their journeys accordingly. This information will enable them to make better use of their time and optimize their travel plans.

Say Goodbye to Waiting Time.

We are here to provide a great user experience for customers who love to travel on public transport. Users can easily access their routes, find out estimated departure times for their bus stops, schedule their trips, and make online payments. They are also able to track buses live within a radius of their bus stop. This feature is helpful for people to plan their trips accordingly and helps reduce overcrowding on buses. 

 
 
 
 

Journey & security: To begin, please enter your current location and the desired drop-off location for your bus journey.We have designed our system with a strong emphasis on user security. In the profile section, you can easily access emergency contacts, such as the security team and nearby hospitals, with just one tap. This feature provides quick assistance in case of any emergency situations.

Day 1: understanding the problem

Volunteer to Develop Empathy.

Due to time constraints, I was not able to volunteer as my preferred way of research. However, to be resourceful I looked up other bus stops, RTC buses to develop empathy and understand the daily grind of service they provide to customers.

 
 

When buses do not arrive on time at bus stops, people can feel frustrated, inconvenienced, and disappointed. It can disrupt their schedules, cause them to be late for appointments, and create unnecessary stress. When buses consistently fail to adhere to their schedules, it erodes the trust and confidence of the public in the transportation system, leading to dissatisfaction and a negative perception of the service provider.

Based on the interview responses I received from Neha, a student who relies on public transport to commute to her college, it is evident that she frequently experiences frustration due to changes in bus timings and overcrowding. These issues contribute to her dissatisfaction with the use of public transport. Through further investigation, I have discovered that one of the primary goals for people using public transport is to arrive at their destinations on time. Therefore, implementing a live tracking system for buses would greatly benefit the public. Taking these considerations into account, I have also formulated a set of additional questions to gather more insights from public transport users:

  1. How often have you experienced changes in bus timings while using public transport?

  2. In your opinion, what measures or improvements could be implemented to address the challenges of changing bus timings and overcrowding? 

  3. How important is it for you to have access to real-time bus tracking information? How would it benefit your travel planning and overall experience?

  4. What other features or services would you like to see implemented in public transport systems to enhance the overall user experience?

Reflections + What I would do differently

  • Be cautious, but never assume. As it is my goal to design with as much empathy as possible, this project made me learn to be extremely sensitive with topics that deal with economic or social disparities.

  • Experiencing the problem first hand is the best research. Although I was not able to collaborate with all customers of public who travel, to the extent I desired, it turned into a riveting exercise to empathize with what it is like to be a traveler in public transport without the first-hand interview.

Overall, I have sent the design file to the "TSRTC" team I hope to take the lessons learned in this project towards more inclusive and empowering design in my community. Onward and upward!
