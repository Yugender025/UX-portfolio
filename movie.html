The Problem in my hometown of Hyderabad HYD

On any given day on average over 2 lakhs people in Hyderabad are watch movies in theatre. Not all people in the group are well educated, so they may have difficulty understanding the seating arrangements in the theatre. It could be confusing for them Cinematic where workers need to assit them all the time to their seats it becomes problem to them.

However, workers assit the customers and provide good movie experience to them.

A Solution for easy acces to their seats.

The goal of a mobile ticketing app for a movie theater is to offer customers a seamless and convenient method to purchase tickets while ensuring a good user experience. This eliminates the need for customers to wait in long lines at the theater. Additionally, the app can provide notifications regarding their ticket purchase, including seat mapping to assist those who may find seating arrangements confusing. It can also offer reminders about showtimes and any changes to the schedule .

Day 1: understanding the problem

Volunteer to Develop Empathy.

To better understand our target audience and their requirements, we performed user research. We surveyed and interviewed actual moviegoers and examined data from existing movie ticketing applications. We went into the research with some assumptions, such as that users were primarily interested in ease and speed when purchasing tickets, but we discovered that customers also enjoyed the option to pick their seats and inspect the theatre layout before purchasing tickets.

 
 

Moreover, we discovered that customers want the opportunity to see trailers and read reviews before choosing on a movie, as well as the ability to receive real-time updates and alerts regarding their ticket purchases and showtimes. Overall, the user research assisted us in better understanding the requirements and preferences of our customers.

Based on the interview responses from Arjun, a worker at the theater, and a movie watcher, it appears that there are issues related to seat finding and the mobile app experience. These are some of the questions I asked:

  1. How easy or difficult was it for you to navigate through the app?

  2. Could you describe the process of selecting seats within the app? 

  3. Any specific elements that you found visually appealing or distracting?

  4. Were there any specific features or functionalities you felt were missing or could be improved to enhance your experience with the app?

Reflections + What I would do differently

  • Be cautious, but never assume. As it is my goal to design with as much empathy as possible, this project made me learn to be extremely sensitive with topics that deal with economic or social disparities.

  • Experiencing the problem first hand is the best research. Although I was not able to collaborate with Movie ticketing apps to the extent I desired, it turned into a riveting exercise to empathize with what it is like to be a customer of theatre without the first-hand interview.

Overall, I have sent the design file to the Cinematic team I hope to take the lessons learned in this project towards more inclusive and empowering design in my community. Onward and upward!
